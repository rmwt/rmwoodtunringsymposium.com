Turning a Natural Edge Burl Vase and enhancement with aniline dye

I will be turning a small natural edge burl vase utilizing Trent Bosch turning tools and other basic tools. After completing the turning, I will enhance the burl grain with aniline dye. The dye technique will include use of multiple colors for a bold and dynamic effect.

Turning a Footed End-grain Bowl

Not the typical orientation of a bowl blank, the turning will done on the end-grain of the blank. The project techniques follow a demonstration/class presented by Michael Gibson and I have but my own spin on the hallowing process. The turning process utilizes basic turning tools and the challenges present by end-grain bowl turning. The unique foot on the bowl adds to the finished project.

Hands-on � Simplified Hallowing

The hands-on project will walk through the hallowing process in the completion of a pen & pencil cup. This project allows the participant to utilize a variety of hollowing tools, while being able to observe the cutting action inside the vessel. The project will help the beginner try their hand at hallowing and for the more advanced turner to prefect their cut and continuity of wall thickness.
